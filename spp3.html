Place the tree outdoors, all year round, in a place that receives plenty of sunlight. The Juniper is unable to live inside. Once it drops below 15 °F (-10 °C), preserve the tree during the winter by putting it in a coldframe, for instance. Some species' foliage turns purplish brown during frosty weather as a part of their natural defense against the cold. They will become green once more in the spring, so don't worry.
